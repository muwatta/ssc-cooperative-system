For Client & Developer Review Only  Page 2 / 17  1.  System Purpose & Cooperative Structure  
SOLACE STAFF COOPERATIVE LTD (SMS) is a sub -unit of the school’s Accounting Department. It manages 
voluntary staff savings, loan applications, suretyship, termly dues, and investment profit -sharing for all school staff 
members across branches.  
 
Staff remit their monthly savings contribution through the school payslip system. The payslip carries the savings 
contribution field only. All other cooperative activities — loans, repayments, dues, investments — are managed 
entirely within this platform.  
 
Key Operational Boundary  
Payslip carries ONE cooperative field: the approved monthly savings amount.  
Loan repayments are posted manually inside this system by Admin or Committee.  
Termly dues are posted by Admin and debited directly from member savings.  
The cooperative keeps its own ledgers, independent of the school payroll figures.  
 
1.1  Calendar System  
The SMS uses the Islamic (Hijri) calendar as its primary record -keeping calendar. All savings entries, loan 
records, repayment schedules, and dues are dated by Islamic month and year. The Gregorian date is recorded 
alongside as a secondary reference.  
 
Islamic Month  Gregorian Approx.  
Muharram, Safar, Rabi’ al -
Awwal, Rabi’ al -Thani  January – April (approx.)  
Jumada al -Ula, Jumada al -
Akhira, Rajab, Sha’ban  April – August (approx.)  
Ramadan, Shawwal, Dhul 
Qa‘da, Dhul Hijja  August – December (approx.)  
 
The system stores both calendars. Admin selects the Islamic month when posting any entry. The Gregorian date 
is auto -recorded by the system timestamp.  
 
1.2  School Branches  
Members belong to specific school branches (e.g. Primary, College). Branch is recorded on the membership form 
and stored in the member profile. Reports can be filtered by branch.  
 
1.3  How the Cooperative Operates  
• Every staff member voluntarily joins SMS by completing a Membership Form.  
• Each month, the member contributes an approved savings amount through the payslip.  
• The monthly amount is set via a formal Savings Increase/Decrease Form approved by the Chairman.  
• Savings accumulate and build each member’s cooperative balance over time.  
• Members may borrow from the cooperative fund subject to eligibility rules.  
• Termly dues are periodically deducted from member savings by Admin.  
• Investment profits are shared proportionally among contributing members.

6.1  Six -Layer Structure  
Layer  Who Coverage Rule  
1 
(Mandatory)  Borrower — Self 
Surety  Must cover 75% of loan from own Available Balance. Non -negotiable 
first layer.  
2 External Surety 1  Up to 75% of their own Available Balance committed as guarantee.  
3 External Surety 2  Up to 75% of their own Available Balance committed as guarantee.  
4 External Surety 3  Up to 75% of their own Available Balance committed as guarantee.  
5 External Surety 4  Up to 75% of their own Available Balance committed as guarantee.  
6 External Surety 5  Up to 75% of their own Available Balance committed as guarantee.  
 
External sureties 2 through 5 are optional. Only Surety 1 (self) is mandatory. The combined external sureties must 
together cover the full shortfall beyond the self -surety portion.  
 
6.2  Surety Eligibility & Limits  
Rule Detail  
Savings tenure  Surety must have 6 consecutive months of savings.  
Available balance check  Surety’s Available Balance must be ≥ the amount they are guaranteeing.  
Maximum commitment  Each external surety may commit up to 75% of their Available Balance.  
Annual limit  A member may act as external surety a maximum of 3 times per 
calendar year.  
Confirmation  Each surety must individually confirm acceptance before loan is 
approved.  
Membership requirement  All sureties must be members of the SMS society.  
 
6.3  Surety Locking, Release & Default  
• Committed amount is locked immediately upon surety confirmation, reducing their Available Balance.  
• As borrower repays each month, each surety’s liability reduces proportionally.  
• When loan balance reaches ₦0 — all sureties are fully and automatically released.  
• If borrower defaults OR exits employment abruptly — outstanding balance transfers immediately to sureties 
in proportion to their original guarantee amount.  
• Sureties are then obligated to service the remaining debt.

All rules below are enforced by the backend system at the API level. No user interface action can bypass them.  
 
7.1  Savings Rules  
# Rule 
S1 Minimum monthly savings contribution is ₦1,000. Entries below this are rejected by the system.  
S2 A member’s monthly contribution is their last approved amount from the Savings Increase/Decrease 
Form.  
S3 Savings are posted by Admin only, through the payslip savings field.  
S4 A member’s savings balance can never go below zero.  
S5 If a non -debtor misses a month: savings balance is frozen. No addition, no subtraction. Consecutive 
counter resets.  
S6 Active debtors cannot skip a repayment month. System requires a repayment posting every month 
from Admin/Committee.  
S7 Termly dues are posted by Admin and debited from member savings. Members cannot dispute or skip 
dues.  
 
7.2  Membership Rules  
# Rule 
M1 New members must complete 6 consecutive months of savings before becoming eligible for loans or 
suretyship.  
M2 Missing any savings month resets the consecutive counter to zero. The count restarts from the next 
posted month.  
M3 All sureties must be active, eligible SMS members.  
M4 If a member exits employment while owing, the outstanding balance transfers immediately to their 
sureties.  
 
7.3  Loan Rules  
# Rule 
L1 Maximum borrowable is 75% of the member’s Available Balance.  
L2 A member may not have more than one active loan at any time.  
L3 A member with active suretyship liabilities may not apply for a new loan.  
L4 Maximum 4 approved loan applications per member per calendar year.  
L5 Repayment schedule must be structured to clear within 12 months at application time.  
L6 Hard cap: the system blocks any repayment schedule proposed beyond 12 months.  
L7 Repayments are posted manually by Admin or Committee. Never deducted from payslip.  
L8 Admin repayment actions supersede Committee actions in any conflict.  
L9 Admin may apply for loans under the exact same rules as staff. Admin cannot approve their own loan.  
L10 All loans require loan committee chairman approval followed by Head of School final sign -off.
